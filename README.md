# Global Basic Ledger Protocol (GBLP)

**A Dual-Layer Supervised Digital Currency System with a Universal Basic Wallet for Every Human**

GBLP proposes a practical, evolution-ready global monetary architecture that solves the trilemma of sovereignty, stability, and individual dignity in the digital age.

## Core Components

### 1. Regional Currencies
- Issued and managed by national governments or regional monetary unions.
- Used for daily payments, wages, taxes, and local investments.
- Fully sovereign: each government sets its own monetary policy.

### 2. Global Settlement Coin (GSC)
- Used only for cross-border trade, large investments, and global reserves.
- Dynamically anchored to a basket of verifiable physical goods and services (commodities, energy, computing power).
- Issued via proof-of-real-assets – no government can mint it arbitrarily.
- Managed by a Central Bank Alliance (CBA).

### 3. Earth Basic Wallet (EBW)
- One non-deprivable wallet per human being.
- Monthly basic unit amount = **80% of the local survival line** (adjusted per region).
- The amount is **below subsistence** – everyone must work for a better life.
- Cannot be seized or frozen by any government (with narrow, court-ordered exceptions).
- Funded initially by voluntary contributions from developed regions, then sustained by a 0.1% global solidarity tax on GSC exchanges and seigniorage.

## Key Mechanisms

- **Exchange**: Regional currencies ↔ GSC via staked liquidity pools. Exchange rate determined algorithmically based on local fundamentals.
- **Anti-abuse**: Progressive penalty for basic unit reselling (up to 6 months suspension).
- **Sanctions**: If a government blocks EBW access – citizens’ wallets become protected hibernation; top decision-makers lose their personal EBW for 3 years.
- **Assistance**: Developed nations contribute surplus **new or 80%+ functional goods** (during crises) via on-chain vouchers.
