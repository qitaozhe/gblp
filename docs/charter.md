# Governance Charter of the Global Digital Currency Committee (Draft)

## Preamble

In light of the need for a fair, transparent, and accountable governance mechanism to ensure the stable operation of the dual‑layer system of Earth Basic Wallet, Global Settlement Coin, and Regional Currencies, while respecting national sovereignty and cultural diversity, the **Global Digital Currency Governance Committee** (hereinafter “the Committee”) is established. This Charter is the supreme binding document; no member or institution may violate it.

---

## Chapter Zero: The Eternal Coin – Unamendable Pre‑Consensus

### Article 0: Definition and Status of the Eternal Coin

(1) The **Eternal Coin (symbol Ξ)** is the highest symbol and moral axiom of the GBLP system. It is not a tradable digital asset, has no balance, and does not participate in any exchange or payment. Its sole purpose is to serve as the ultimate inviolable reference for all rules.

(2) The total supply of the Eternal Coin is **1**, indivisible, non‑inflationary, and indestructible. Its “holder” is defined as **all of humanity** (collectively symbolised by the set of all Earth Basic Wallets).

(3) The three principles represented by the Eternal Coin shall not be violated by any institution, government, or smart contract:
- **Integrity of Earth**: no destruction of ecosystems, no environmental catastrophe, no irreversible overexploitation of resources.
- **Safety of Humanity**: no large‑scale war, no genocide, no systematic starvation or humanitarian disaster.
- **Permanence of Public Trust**: no systematic fraud, no deletion of global public ledgers, no undermining of intergenerational commitments.

(4) All other provisions of this Charter, as well as the rules for minting the Global Settlement Coin, exchanging Regional Currencies, and sanctions, are premised on this Article. **Article 0 of this Charter shall not be amended, suspended, or repealed by any majority vote.**

### Article 0.1: Emergency Trigger Conditions for the Eternal Coin

When any of the following conditions is confirmed for 7 consecutive days by at least three independent data sources (e.g., UN, WMO, International Peace Institute, Global Trust Index survey), a **State of Eternal Emergency** shall be deemed triggered:

1. **Climate Tipping Point**: Global average surface temperature exceeds +1.5 °C above pre‑industrial levels and remains above that threshold for 12 consecutive months; or an irreversible tipping event (e.g., ice sheet collapse, AMOC shutdown) recognised by scientific consensus occurs.
2. **War and Atrocity Threshold**: Any single armed conflict causes more than 100,000 civilian deaths within one year; or genocide as confirmed by the International Criminal Court.
3. **Global Trust Collapse**: The “Global Trust Index” (jointly published by Gallup, World Values Survey, and independent auditors) falls below 30% (on a 0–100 scale) for two consecutive quarters.

### Article 0.2: Automatic Measures During the State of Emergency

Once the State of Eternal Emergency is triggered, the following measures shall take effect automatically, without any committee vote, and no institution may interfere:

#### Phase One (Days 1–30 after trigger)
a) All regional‑currency‑to‑GSC exchange pools enter a **“watch period”**: settlement delay extended to 2 hours (instead of real‑time) to slow abnormal capital flows.  
b) The global solidarity tax (normally 0.1%) is temporarily raised to 0.3%, with the additional revenue fully allocated to the “Earth Basic Wallet Emergency Reserve” for humanitarian aid.  
c) Any pending proposals to adjust the GSC real‑goods basket are frozen until the emergency is lifted.

#### Phase Two (Days 31–180 after trigger)
a) An **Eternal Coin Interim Review Committee** (as defined in Article 0.3) is automatically formed.  
b) That Committee may, by a 2/3 majority, **suspend** the exchange eligibility of any country whose actions are deemed to severely violate the principles of the Eternal Coin.  
c) All new minting of GSC requires double collateral of verified goods, and the goods must be “green‑certified” (low‑carbon production evidence).

#### Phase Three (After 180 days if the emergency continues)
a) **Global Monetary Reset Compact** automatically activated: the reference anchor for all regional‑currency‑to‑GSC exchange rates is temporarily switched from the real‑goods basket to a “minimum subsistence basket” (prioritising universal basic allowance).  
b) Any country attempting to withdraw from the system during this period shall see its citizens’ Earth Basic Wallets funded directly by the global solidarity tax (bypassing the national government).  
c) The original Committee is dissolved; the Eternal Coin Interim Review Committee exercises all governance powers until the emergency is lifted and a new election is held.

### Article 0.3: Composition of the Eternal Coin Interim Review Committee

- Seven members:
  - 2 nominated by the UN Secretary‑General (climate/humanitarian experts);
  - 2 scientists nominated by the WMO and IPCC;
  - 1 judge nominated by the International Criminal Court;
  - 2 citizen representatives directly elected by global Earth Basic Wallet users.
- Each member must publicly sign the **Eternal Coin Pledge**, declaring that they will place the principles of the Eternal Coin above any national interest.
- Decisions require a 5/7 majority. All meeting records must be fully public.

### Article 0.4: Lifting the State of Emergency

When all conditions that triggered the emergency (as per Article 0.1) have ceased to exist for 30 consecutive days, the Eternal Coin Interim Review Committee shall vote to lift the emergency (5/7 majority required). After lifting, all temporary measures shall be gradually withdrawn, and the normal rules of the monetary system shall be restored. Suspended exchange privileges shall be re‑examined by the original Committee before reinstatement.

## Chapter I: Composition and Seats of the Committee

### Article 1: Three Categories of Members

| Category | Selection Method | Number of Seats | Voting Weight Basis |
|----------|------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Regional Representatives** | Each participating country or region (including non‑sovereign entities such as Hong Kong, Puerto Rico) appoints one representative. Regions with population below 1 million may jointly appoint one. | Up to 198 (adjusted by UN membership) | One person, one vote (each regional rep has 1 vote) |
| **Technical Experts** | Nominated by ITU, ISO, IACR, etc., confirmed by general vote. Term: 5 years, maximum two consecutive terms. | 21 (permanent) | Each has 1 vote, but counted separately from regional votes (see Chapter III) |
| **Citizen Observers** | Directly elected by active Earth Basic Wallet users (one observer per 10 million active wallets). Term: 3 years, renewable once. | Dynamic (~80 for 8 billion people) | Advisory only, no final vote, but can initiate reconsideration |

### Article 2: Geographical Balance

- Each continent must have at least 10% of regional representative seats. If insufficient, the most populous country of that continent temporarily provides additional representatives.
- No single country or bloc (e.g., the EU) may control more than 30% of regional representative seats.

---

## Chapter II: Decision Scope and Voting Mechanisms

### Article 3: Matters Requiring Full Committee Vote (simultaneously 2/3 majority of regional reps + simple majority of technical experts)

1. Adjusting the global formula for basic wallet allowance (composition of survival line, percentage, etc.).
2. Approving changes to the GSC real‑goods basket (adding or removing a commodity).
3. Sanctioning a government for blocking Earth Basic Wallet access (determination and penalties).
4. Amending this Charter.

### Article 4: Matters Requiring Only Simple Majority of Regional Representatives (>50%)

1. Approving a new country or region to join the system.
2. Determining the annual administrative budget (from a portion of the global solidarity tax).
3. Electing the Committee Chair (2‑year term, rotating, non‑renewable).

### Article 5: Matters Decided Independently by Technical Experts (subject to 7‑day public notice, and effective only if no collective objection from regional representatives)

1. Algorithm parameters for minting/redemption of GSC (e.g., IoT standards for warehouse receipt verification).
2. Updates to the abuse‑detection AI model.
3. Emergency security patches.

## Chapter III: Initiation and Enforcement of Sanctions

### Article 6: Sanctions for Individual Reselling of Basic Units

- Automatically enforced by smart contract (see technical whitepaper). No committee vote required. However, the sanctioned individual may appeal to the **Arbitration Tribunal** under the Committee. The Tribunal shall consist of 3 technical experts and 2 citizen observers; a 2/3 majority may overturn the penalty.

### Article 7: Sanctions for a Government Banning Earth Basic Wallet Access

- **Trigger Condition**: Any government systematically prevents its citizens from accessing the Earth Basic Wallet for more than 7 days through laws, decrees, or de facto actions.
- **Determination Procedure**:
  1. A citizen observer or regional representative files a complaint.
  2. Technical experts produce a forensic report within 48 hours (evidence of network blocks, GPS spoofing, etc.).
  3. Full committee vote (as per Charter Article 3).
- **Specific Sanctions**:
  - All citizens’ wallets of that country enter “protected hibernation” until the blockade is lifted (see technical whitepaper).
  - For the **leaders and cabinet members who directly ordered the blockade**: their personal Earth Basic Wallets are suspended for **3 years**. If the blockade lasts longer than 90 days, the suspension extends to 5 years.
  - The country’s regional exchange pool is throttled by 50% (settlement delay of 2 hours per exchange), increasing transaction costs.
- **Lifting Sanctions**: When the country’s government publicly revokes the blockade and restores access within 30 days, citizens’ wallets automatically receive all back‑owed allowances. The suspension for officials continues for the remaining period but expires automatically afterwards.

### Article 8: Sanctions for Fraudulent Real‑Goods Aid by Donor Countries

- First violation: warning and public disclosure.
- Second violation: suspension of goods‑aid eligibility for 6 months (only GSC donations allowed).
- Third violation: suspension for 12 months, and the country’s regional representative voting weight is reduced to 0.5 (from 1).

## Chapter IV: Relationship with Existing International Organisations

### Article 9: Relation with BIS and IMF

- The Committee does not replace the BIS or IMF but signs **complementary agreements**:
  - BIS continues to oversee cross‑border settlement of existing fiat systems; the Committee supervises the exchange between Regional Currencies and GSC.
  - IMF SDR may serve as one of the references for GSC valuation, but not as a peg.
  - The Committee invites BIS and IMF to each appoint a permanent observer (non‑voting).

### Article 10: Relation with the United Nations

- The Committee is recognised by the UN General Assembly as a special consultative body and submits an annual report.
- The Committee shall implement Security Council resolutions concerning international financial security (e.g., freezing wallets linked to terrorism).

### Article 11: Sovereignty Reservation Clause

- Any country joining the system fully retains its monetary sovereignty (issuing its own regional currency, setting exchange rate policies, etc.). The Committee may not interfere with its domestic monetary policy.
- The Committee’s only compulsory powers are over **access to the Earth Basic Wallet and the rules of the GSC exchange pool**.

## Chapter V: Withdrawal Mechanism

### Article 12: State Withdrawal

- A country may give 6 months’ notice to withdraw from the Committee. Upon withdrawal, its citizens’ Earth Basic Wallets become “inactive” (balance preserved but no new allowances granted). The country may still use the regional‑currency‑to‑GSC exchange pool but must post a higher collateral (20% instead of the normal 10%).
- If it later rejoins, it must follow the full accession procedure and is not eligible for regional representative seats for 2 years.

### Article 13: Individual Withdrawal

- An individual may request closure of their Earth Basic Wallet. The remaining balance shall be donated to the Global Solidarity Fund. Closure is irreversible. To re‑establish a wallet, a new registration is required (with all prior history reset).

## Chapter VI: Entry into Force and Amendment

### Article 14: Entry into Force

- This Charter shall enter into force provisionally when ratified by at least **60 sovereign states** (including at least 3 permanent members of the UN Security Council) and by **30% or more of pre‑registered Earth Basic Wallet users** (expressed via digital signature consent).
- Within 5 years of provisional entry into force, full ratification by national parliaments or referenda shall be completed.

### Article 15: Amendment Procedure

- Any amendment must simultaneously satisfy:
  - 2/3 majority of regional representatives (with opposing countries not exceeding 20% of total seats);
  - 4/5 majority of technical experts;
  - 2/3 majority of citizen observers (observers have no veto but may return a proposal for reconsideration once).

## Appendix: Definitions

- **Earth Basic Wallet (EBW)**: non‑deprivable digital right for every human, monthly allowance set at 80% of local survival line.
- **Global Settlement Coin (GSC)**: real‑goods‑basket‑anchored coin for international trade and reserves.
- **Regional Currency**: national or sub‑national digital currency used for daily payments.
- **Protected Hibernation**: temporary freeze of EBW due to government blockade, with full historical data preserved.
