# FAQ – Common Objections and Responses

## 1. “This violates monetary sovereignty!”
**Response**: No – countries keep full control over regional currencies. They only lose the right to ban EBW, which is a human dignity floor, not a monetary tool.

## 2. “Basic units will make people lazy.”
**Response**: The amount is 80% of survival – not enough for a decent life. Studies on UBI show no significant drop in labor participation. Anyone wanting more must work.

## 3. “Why should the rich world pay?”
**Response**: Initial contributions are voluntary; long-term funding comes from a 0.1% tax on GSC exchanges (mostly used by large corporations). It buys global stability – fewer refugees, less extremism.

## 4. “How to prevent duplicate identities?”
**Response**: Progressive enrollment – national IDs first; then community-witnessed temporary IDs; then biometric checks. Fraud rates can be kept very low.

## 5. “What if a government jams satellites?”
**Response**: Offline mesh networks (Bluetooth) still work. Jamming is costly and illegal. The economic sanctions (exchange pool throttling, leader penalties) make such actions politically suicidal.

## 6. “Six months of no basic units could starve someone.”
**Response**: Suspension only stops new basic units – the user can still receive transfers from others (family, friends). Emergency relief can restore 50% if extreme hardship is proven. Only repeated resellers reach the 6-month cap.

## 7. “Isn’t this wildly utopian?”
**Response**: The goal is not to implement tomorrow, but to have a ready blueprint when the next crisis (AI unemployment, climate-driven migration) forces a global rethink. This is a lifeboat, not a daily driver.
