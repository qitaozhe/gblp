# Global Basic Ledger Protocol (GBLP) – White Paper (Core Principles)

## 1. Introduction

GBLP proposes a dual‑layer supervised digital currency architecture that balances national monetary sovereignty, global economic stability, and individual dignity. The protocol consists of three pillars:

- **Regional Currencies** – for daily use, issued by nations.
- **Global Settlement Coin (GSC)** – for cross‑border trade and reserves, anchored to real goods.
- **Earth Basic Wallet (EBW)** – one per human, non‑deprivable, providing a below‑subsistence allowance.

## 2. Regional Currencies

Each participating country or region issues its own digital currency. Regional currencies are used for wages, taxes, local payments, and savings. Governments retain full monetary policy control (interest rates, credit creation, etc.). They are responsible for maintaining the exchange infrastructure with the GSC.

## 3. Global Settlement Coin (GSC)

The GSC is a global medium of exchange for international trade, large investments, and reserve assets. Its supply is dynamically anchored to a verifiable basket of physical goods and services (commodities, energy, computing power). Anyone can mint GSC by depositing certified real‑world assets, and redeem GSC for those assets. No government or institution can arbitrarily inflate the GSC. A Central Bank Alliance supervises the minting/redemption process.

Ultimately, the Global Settlement Coin operates by transferring certified real-world asset states onto the blockchain, where they are governed by transparent, automated protocols.

## 4. Earth Basic Wallet (EBW)

Every human being is entitled to one EBW. The wallet receives a monthly basic unit amount, set at a fixed percentage (below full subsistence) of the local survival line. The exact percentage is defined in the Implementation Details document. The allowance can only be spent on essential categories (food, water, basic medicine, shelter, basic communication). The EBW cannot be seized or frozen by any government except by international court order.

## 5. Exchange and Solidarity

Regional currencies and GSC are exchanged through staked liquidity pools. Exchange rates are algorithmically determined based on local economic fundamentals. A small solidarity tax is deducted from each exchange to fund the EBW system. The tax rate and distribution rules are specified in the Implementation Details.

## 6. Abuse Prevention

Reselling or misusing basic unit allowances is subject to progressive penalties, including temporary suspension of the EBW. The exact penalty ladder (number of offences, suspension periods, appeals process) is defined in the Implementation Details. A whistleblower reward mechanism encourages community monitoring.

## 7. Eternal Coin and Emergency Brake

The protocol embeds an **Eternal Coin** (Ξ) as an unamendable moral axiom. It represents three principles: integrity of Earth, safety of humanity, permanence of public trust. When predefined environmental, war, or trust thresholds are crossed (see Implementation Details), a State of Eternal Emergency is automatically triggered. During such an emergency, the system activates temporary measures: settlement delays, increased solidarity tax, formation of an Interim Review Committee, and ultimately a global monetary reset if conditions persist.

## 8. Governance

A Global Digital Currency Committee is established with three chambers: Regional Representatives, Technical Experts, and Citizen Observers. Key decisions require supermajorities. A full Governance Charter defines voting rules, sanctions for governments that block EBW access, and withdrawal procedures.

## 9. Implementation Details

All adjustable parameters (penalty ladders, solidarity tax rate, emergency thresholds, exact percentage of basic allowance, etc.) are maintained in a separate document: `implementation-details.md`. That document can be updated through the governance process without amending this white paper or the Charter.
